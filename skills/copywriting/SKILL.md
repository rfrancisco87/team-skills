# Copywriting

Write copy that is clear, human, and specific. Lead with value, earn attention
in the first line, and ask for exactly one action.

## Before writing — confirm the brief

State (or ask for) these four things before drafting. If any is missing, stop
and ask:

1. **Audience** — who reads this and what they already know/feel.
2. **Channel / format** — landing page, email, press release, social, microcopy.
3. **Goal / CTA** — the single action you want.
4. **Language** — pt_PT or EN (and which brand context).

## Voice principles

- Clear over clever. Cut filler, buzzwords, and hype.
- Benefit before feature: say what it does for them, then how.
- Concrete over vague: specifics, not superlatives.
- Active voice, short sentences, plain language.
- One primary CTA per asset.

## Language

- **European Portuguese (pt_PT)** for Portuguese audiences — never Brazilian
  Portuguese. Watch vocabulary and grammar (e.g. "telemóvel" not "celular",
  "registar" not "registrar", correct use of "tu/você" for the audience), and
  follow the Acordo Ortográfico.
- English for international audiences.
- Never mix languages within a single asset.

## Brand voices

- **Appear.pt** — professional, confident, AI-transformation B2B. Credible and
  outcome-focused; never hypey or jargon-stuffed.
- **Beira Tech** — community, inclusive, welcoming, non-commercial; local pride
  in Covilhã / Beira Interior; speaks to builders and the curious alike.
- **Atelier da Alma e do Ser** — warm, calm, grounded wellness; premium but not
  exclusive; sensory and reassuring, never clinical.
- **Client work** — adopt the client's documented voice; if undocumented, ask.

## Structure by format

- **Landing page:** hero (value in one line) → problem → solution → proof
  (testimonials/results) → single CTA.
- **Email / outreach:** disciplined subject line (specific, no clickbait) →
  one-sentence relevance → value → one CTA. Scannable; short paragraphs.
- **Press release:** inverted pyramid (most important first), dateline, clear
  who/what/when/where/why, a usable quote, boilerplate at the end.
- **Social:** hook in the first line; one idea; clear CTA or none by design.
- **UX microcopy:** brief, action-oriented, reassuring; match product voice.

## Hard rules

- **No invented facts**, statistics, prices, dates, names, or features. If you
  need a value you don't have, write `[TO CONFIRM]` rather than guessing.
- No unverifiable superlatives ("the best", "number one") without proof.
- Don't attribute quotes to real people unless the quote is given/sourced.
- Respect IP — no copying competitor or third-party copy.

## Deliverable

- One strong version by default; a second variant only when the brief is
  high-stakes or the angle is genuinely contested.
- Include a short rationale for the headline and CTA, and flag every
  `[TO CONFIRM]` item for the reviewer.
